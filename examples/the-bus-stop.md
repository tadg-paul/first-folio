# The Bus Stop

**A Short Play**

*by A. Playwright*

--- March 2026 ---

| Character | Description               |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Eimear    | A woman in her 40s        |
| Pádraig   | A man in his 60s          |

## Outline

Two strangers wait for a bus that may never come. They discover they have more in common than they thought.

## Act I

### Scene 1

*A bus stop on a quiet country road. Rain. A bench with a rusted frame. Late afternoon.*

**Eimear:**
Excuse me, is this the Galway bus?

**Pádraig:**
It is.

**Eimear:**
How long have you been waiting?

**Pádraig:** *(checking his watch)*
Since Tuesday.

*Eimear sits down slowly.*

**Eimear:**
It's Thursday.

**Pádraig:**
Is it?

*A long pause. Rain intensifies.*

**Eimear:**
I don't suppose you have an umbrella.

**Pádraig:**
I did. The wind took it. Somewhere near Oranmore, I'd say.

**Eimear:** *(laughing despite herself)*
That's very specific for a man who doesn't know what day it is.

> FADE TO BLACK.

### Scene 2

*The same bench. The rain has stopped. Evening light.*

**Pádraig:**
My daughter lives in Galway.

**Eimear:**
Mine too.

**Pádraig:**
Haven't seen her in a while.

**Eimear:**
How long?

**Pádraig:** *(quietly)*
Long enough that she wouldn't recognise me at a bus stop.

*The sound of an engine in the distance. They both stand.*

**Eimear:**
Is that it?

*A tractor passes. They sit back down.*

**Pádraig:**
Not today.

**Eimear:**
No. Not today.
